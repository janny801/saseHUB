Resume Building Guide ▶
Rules - LENGTH: 1 page - FORMATTING: Simple, clean, easy to edit - ORDER: Sections sorted by relevance, items in reverse chronological order - PROJECTS: Reverse chronological or relevance-based - WORK IN PROGRESS: Resume evolves over time - SAVING: Save as PDF with clear file name Recommended Resume Sections 1. Education 2. Skills & Certifications 3. Experience 4. Projects 5. Activities / Leadership Google XYZ Formula “Accomplished X by implementing Y which led to Z” Outline Your Accomplishments - Put strongest bullets first - Show impact (how many, how much, how long) - Quantify results - Keep bullet points short (1–2 lines) Describing Experience Project/Product – What it is and the problem it solves Role – What you did, challenges you solved Achievements – Use XYZ formula Tech stack – (Java, Python, Git, Jenkins, etc.) General Resume Template (Linked PDF in original content)
Coffee Chat 101 ▶
A coffee chat is an informal networking meeting between professionals. Benefits - Strengthen current connections - Reestablish old connections - Make new connections - Learn about opportunities Tips - Take initiative - Prepare beforehand - Arrive early - Follow up Questions to Ask - How did you get started? - What do you enjoy most? - What challenges do you face? - What skills are most important? - What advice do you have for beginners?
Conference Preparation ▶
Upcoming content for conference preparation: - Hospitality Suites prep - Earn potential interviews - 20–30 companies present - Networking at workshops - Approaching high-level managers and executives - Interview experience at SASE National
Setting & Achieving Goals ▶
Short-Term vs Long-Term Goals - Short-term: Achievable soon, supports long-term goals - Long-term: Multi-year achievements Benefits - Guides your career path - Helps maintain focus - Makes large projects manageable - Supports advancement SMART Goals Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-based Tips - Be realistic - Share goals with others - Acknowledge your efforts
Technical Interviews ▶
Preparation Steps - Review job posting & required skills - Practice algorithms and data structures - Solve coding problems - Review your resume projects - Research the company - Mock interviews Resources - HackerRank, LeetCode, CodeForces - Books: DDIA, CTCI, EPI
Creating a LinkedIn ▶
LinkedIn Best Practices Profile - Use a recent, well-lit photo - Add a curated header photo - Featured section: GitHub, portfolio, projects Experience - Copy your resume bullets - Add relevant media - Projects/volunteering can go under experience Education - Degrees + activities + relevant training Certifications - List key certificates (including CodePath) Volunteer Experience - Unpaid but relevant work Skills - Prioritize top 3 technical skills Recommendations - Ask mentors or supervisors Accomplishments - Courses, awards, orgs, projects (only most relevant)
Behavioral Interviews ▶
Preparation - Prepare months ahead - Use STAR method - Practice regularly (at least 1 hr/week) Day-of Tips - Do energizing activities - Eat beforehand - Join meeting early
Writing Cover Letters & Emails ▶
Purpose of a Cover Letter - Explain your fit, value, and motivation Steps 1. Research company values 2. Match job posting skills 3. Select which skills to highlight (strongest stories) Emails - Use meaningful subject lines - Be clear and concise - Clarify assumptions - Keep communication professional
Tips & Tricks ▶
Networking Tips - Ask politely - Follow up within 48 hours - Mention your referrer Online Communication Tips - Use meaningful subject lines - Be clear and concise - Verify assumptions - Communicate about communication
